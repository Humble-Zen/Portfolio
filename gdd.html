"The radio was mostly static but we heard it clearly. A safe place. Food. Walls. People. We don't know if it's real. But we don't have anything else so we're going."
What is the game

Two brothers are stuck in a city full of zombies. The older brother is you. The younger brother follows you around. You heard on the radio that there is a safe haven somewhere north of the city, on top of an office building. You have to get there.

The city is between you and it. You go through it day by day. Every day you move a bit further. Every night you find a safe room and rest. You eat your food, use your bandages, and hope you have enough to make it to tomorrow.

The most important thing is that your brother stays alive. If he dies the game is over. It doesn't matter how you're doing. He is the whole point.

Why this story

I wanted to make a game where the scary part isn't just the zombies. It's the responsibility. You are the older brother. Your younger brother is scared and trusts you completely. Every decision you make do you go through the dangerous area for more food, do you use the last bandage on him or save it for yourself, do you keep moving or wait all of it is about him.

The safe haven might not even be real. The radio signal gets weaker as you go. But you keep going anyway because what else are you going to do.

You (Older Brother)

You're the one with the gun. You make the decisions. You clear the path. You don't get to lose him. That is the rule you set for yourself before you even started walking.

Him (Younger Brother)

He follows you. He searches for supplies when you tell him to. He hides when you tell him to. He gets scared. He tells you when he sees something. He is not a tool. He is your brother.

How it ends

If you both make it to the rooftop of the office building, you win. The safe haven is real. The radio was right. You don't know what happens next but you made it and he's alive.

If your brother dies anywhere along the way, the game ends right there. No checkpoint. No going back. He's gone and the game is over because without him there's no reason to keep going.

A day in the game

Every day has the same shape. You wake up. You look at what you have. You go out. You try to find more. You find the next safe room before it gets too dangerous. You sleep. Then you do it again, a little further into the city.

Morning
Wake up in the safe room
This is the only time you are completely safe. You check your food, your bandages, your bottles. Your brother says something about how he slept or what he heard in the night. The radio might have an update. Then the door opens and it starts again.
Daytime
Move through the city
You walk through streets and buildings trying to get to the next safe room. Zombies are everywhere. Some are wandering. Some are just standing still. Some are eating something and not paying attention. You have to decide when to sneak past, when to throw something to distract them, and when you have to shoot. Shooting is loud. Really loud. Every zombie in the area hears it.
Whenever it feels safe
Send your brother to search
When there are no zombies nearby you can tell your brother to search a spot a shelf, a cabinet, a car. He walks over and digs around for a bit and comes back with whatever was in there. He won't go if he can see a zombie. He just comes back to you and waits. You have to make the area safe first. Or at least safe enough.
End of day
Find the safe room
Every section of the city has a safe room somewhere. A locked shop, a barricaded flat, a storage room with a thick door. When you get both of you inside and the door locks, you're done for the day. You eat. If you have no food both of you feel it in the morning. You sleep. You do it again tomorrow, further in.
What you carry
  • Food you eat it at night. If you have none, both of you wake up weaker. Run out completely and you die slowly.
  • Bandages for when one of you gets hurt. You choose who to use them on. That choice is the game.
  • Bottles you can give them to your brother to throw at zombies. They smash and the zombies near it go check the noise. Buys you time.
  • Torch the battery runs down. In the dark zombies are harder to see and avoid. But they can also spot the torchlight from far away.
You win if...

Both of you reach the rooftop. Your brother is alive. The safe haven was real.

You lose if...

Your brother dies. Or you die. Or you both starve. If your brother dies it's over instantly. No exceptions.

"He doesn't ask where we're going anymore. He just walks behind me and trusts that I know."
What he does

Your brother follows you. He stays just behind and to the side so he's not in your way. When you stop he stops. When you run he runs. He doesn't get in front of you and he doesn't wander off unless you tell him to search something.

He has his own health bar. Zombies can hurt him. Starvation can hurt him. You can heal him with bandages. If his health hits zero the game ends. His health bar is always on screen. You are always watching it.

Things you can tell him to do
  • Follow me brings him back to you from wherever he is. Works from any situation.
  • Hide he finds the nearest hiding spot and crouches. Zombies won't notice him there. Good when a patrol is passing and you both need to go very still.
  • Search that sends him to find supplies in a nearby spot. He'll search it and come back with whatever he finds.
  • Throw a bottle he throws a bottle at the nearest zombie. It smashes and the zombies nearby go over to check. Useful for clearing a path quickly.
  • It's clear tells him to stop hiding and come back to you.
When he gets scared

When too many zombies get close at once, your brother panics. He stops following properly and just runs. He doesn't run towards safety, he just runs. This makes noise. Noise brings more zombies.

You need to get to him quickly and get him back under control before he makes things worse. He is not stupid. He is just scared. If you stay separated for too long he starts getting hurt even without zombies touching him. The panic itself is dangerous.

What he notices

He spots zombies before you do sometimes. He whispers or says "there's one over there." He is your second pair of eyes. He also tells you when he is hurt, when he is hungry, when he thinks a spot looks worth searching. He talks like a kid. He doesn't give you readouts. He just talks.

Where you are going

You start at a hilltop on the edge of the city. You can see the office building from there. It looks far. The city is between you and it. You go through the city day by day until you get to the building, then you go up it floor by floor until you reach the roof.

The Hilltop (Start)
Where you start. You've been here a while. You have a little bit of food and almost no bandages. No bottles. The city looks bad from up here. Then you hear the radio and decide to go. This is where you figure out how everything works before it gets really dangerous.
The Residential Streets
The first part of the city. Houses, parked cars, a corner shop. Not too many zombies but enough that you have to be careful. This is where you really learn how the brother works sending him to search houses, hiding when patrols walk past, figuring out when to throw a bottle and when to just wait. The safe room here is a barricaded house. It feels almost normal inside.
The Industrial Yard
More zombies and more open space which makes it harder to hide. Big warehouses, shipping containers, a loading dock, broken machinery everywhere. Lots of useful stuff inside the warehouses food crates, supply boxes, medical kits left behind by workers. But the open yard between buildings means zombies can spot you from far away. The containers are good for hiding and throwing bottles from. The warehouses are good for supplies but bad for escaping. Some zombies wander the yard in big slow loops which you can learn and use. Others are just standing still in the dark corners of the warehouses waiting for something to walk past.
The Office Building
The last part. You go in at the ground floor and work your way up. Each floor is its own small fight clear enough space to move, find the stairs, keep your brother close on the way up. Once you go through a stairwell door it locks behind you. You cannot go back down. The roof is the end. If you both make it up there, you've done it.
The zombies

They walk around in patterns mostly. They're not smart but they're dangerous. They hear things running is louder than walking, shooting is the loudest thing you can do. If one zombie sees you it somehow alerts the others without making any sound. They just all start moving towards you at once. That part is scary every time.

Some are just eating when you find them and haven't noticed you yet. A zombie that's eating is distracted. A zombie that is already hurt and chasing you will never stop chasing you. You have to deal with those ones, there is no sneaking away from them.

When you kill one it falls in the direction you hit it from. Dead ones stay where they fall. They pile up. In the office building especially this becomes a real problem.

The radio

The radio plays in every safe room. At the start the signal is clear. A voice talks about the safe haven food, walls, people. As you get deeper into the city the signal gets worse. More static. You still catch words. By the time you are in the office building you are not sure if what you are hearing is real or just what you want to hear. But you keep going because you are already there and there is nowhere to go back to.
