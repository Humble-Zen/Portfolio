Be Safe Lily
Act I: The Routine
A father and his young daughter Lily lived a quiet life on a farm. Every day they followed the same simple routine, walking through the fields, harvesting vegetables, and spending time together. These small, repetitive moments defined their bond.
The Fracture
Over time the father developed dementia. His memory began to deteriorate, but fragments of his daily life remained, especially the routine he shared with Lily. Even as reality faded, this pattern persisted. Then a fire broke out on the farm. Lily died.
Act II: The Reconstruction
Unable to process the loss, the father's mind reconstructs a version of reality where Lily is still present. He continues his daily routine, harvesting crops, moving through the farm, preparing to protect her. The world is unstable. The farm is filled with hostile entities: manifestations of perceived threats. The father arms himself and eliminates them, believing he is keeping Lily safe.
The Puzzle: Mechanics as Metaphor
His actions lead him toward a locked space, something his mind has buried. To retrieve the key he must burn a pile of hay. To him this is necessary, clearing danger and doing what must be done. He does not know he has done this before.
Act III: The Unlocking
With the key in hand he unlocks the door. Inside, the illusion breaks. He is confronted with Lily's lifeless body. His mind cannot fully comprehend what he is seeing, it reshapes the moment into something he can act upon. He does what he believes is right. He uses fire once more.
The Loop
In doing so he unknowingly reenacts the very tragedy that took Lily's life. The routine continues. The memory remains incomplete. And the father continues to protect a daughter who is no longer there.

Interaction

IInteractable is a single-method interface. Any MonoBehaviour that implements Interact() gets picked up by the player's screen-center raycast automatically doors, drawers, pickups, burnables, boards. No special-casing per object type in the player script.

The Fire Puzzle
  • Burnable implements IInteractable same detection path as everything else.
  • Phase 1: fuel it with weapon slot 1. Phase 2: ignite with weapon slot 2, but only if isFueled is true. You can't skip the step.
  • The mechanic and the story are the same thing. The act the father thinks clears danger is the act that killed Lily.
Shooting
  • Camera.ScreenPointToRay from screen center bullets always go where the crosshair is.
  • Four coroutines handle GunRecoil, FlashEffect, GunCooldown, and Reload independently. No timer variables cluttering Update().
  • Dirty flag UI P_Gun_UI caches the last bullet/magazine values and only writes to TextMeshPro when they change. No per-frame string allocation.
Quest System
  • QuestData ScriptableObject questID, title, description, List<QuestObjective>. Designers create quests in the Inspector with no code.
  • Objectives update via events, not polling.
FPS Feel
  • Head bob on sine/cosine wave tied to speed Y for bounce, X for sway at half frequency. It reads as natural without being nauseating.
  • Hysteresis on isMoving higher threshold to start than to stop, so micro-inputs don't cause the bob to stutter on and off.
  • Random footstep clip selection with pitch variation (0.9–1.1) so the same loop doesn't become grating over a 10-minute session.
Enemies

Same Advanced Zombie AI system from my standalone project 8-state FSM, tiered sound detection, squad alerting. Here they're framed as manifestations of the father's paranoia rather than literal zombies.
